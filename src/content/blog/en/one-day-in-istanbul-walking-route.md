You can't "finish" Istanbul in a day — but with the right route you can feel its soul in one. This is a 12-stop, mostly-on-foot itinerary that starts in the historic Old City and ends with sunset in Galata. Total walking distance: about 8 kilometers, with an optional ferry break in between.

## Morning: The Old City (Stops 1–6)

### 1. Sultanahmet Square

Start early — at 8 a.m. the square is still quiet and the light is perfect for photos. Once the Byzantine hippodrome, it still holds the Obelisk of Theodosius and the Serpent Column.

### 2. The Blue Mosque (Sultanahmet Mosque)

Right next to the square. The tens of thousands of blue İznik tiles inside gave it its name. Visit outside prayer times; dress modestly (shoulders and knees covered).

### 3. Hagia Sophia

Standing under a 1,500-year-old dome means seeing Istanbul's layers in a single building: Byzantine mosaics and Ottoman calligraphy share the same space.

### 4. Basilica Cistern

An underground Byzantine cistern held up by 336 columns. Cool, dim and otherworldly — also a great escape from the midday heat. Don't miss the Medusa-head columns.

### 5. Grand Bazaar

One of the world's oldest covered markets: 60+ streets and thousands of shops. Even if you're not shopping, walking its domed alleys is an experience in itself.

### 6. Süleymaniye Mosque

Sinan the Architect's masterpiece sits on a hill overlooking the Golden Horn. The courtyard view of Galata is the best midday photo on this route, and the old Vefa and Zeyrek streets nearby are worth a detour.

## Afternoon: Eminönü and the water (Stops 7–9)

### 7. The Spice Bazaar

Smaller and tidier than the Grand Bazaar, and full of the smell of spices. The right address for dried fruit, Turkish delight and saffron.

### 8. Eminönü waterfront & Galata Bridge

Walk toward Karaköy across the bridge lined with anglers. Fish restaurants below, Bosphorus views above — one of the liveliest cross-sections of the city.

### 9. A short ferry hop (optional)

If you have time, take a quick Eminönü–Üsküdar round trip. No Istanbul day is complete without seeing the city from the water. Seagulls will expect some simit.

## Evening: Galata & Karaköy (Stops 10–12)

### 10. Karaköy's streets

Third-wave coffee shops, galleries and street art in one of the city's fastest-changing districts. Take a coffee break here.

### 11. Galata Tower

A steep but short climb. The streets around the tower come alive toward sunset. Even if you skip going up, the square below is worth lingering in.

### 12. Finish on İstiklal Avenue

Walk from Tünel toward Taksim: the nostalgic tram, historic arcades and Çiçek Pasajı close the day. The Asmalımescit streets next door are full of dinner options.

## Practical tips

- **Shoes:** the route is mostly cobblestone — comfortable shoes are non-negotiable.
- **Timing:** allow 3–4 hours total for museum visits; go to Hagia Sophia early to skip lines.
- **Transport:** nearly the whole route is walkable; the T1 tram follows the same line if you tire.
- **Fridays:** mosques close to visitors around midday prayers — plan accordingly.
